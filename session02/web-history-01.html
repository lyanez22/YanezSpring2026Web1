EARLY 20C, Mundaneum, Paul Otlet and Henri La Fontaine

The Mundaneum was an institution founded in 1910 by Belgian lawyers and bibliographers Paul Otlet and Henri La Fontaine. It aimed to collect and organize all human knowledge through a vast repository of documents, books, and other materials. The Mundaneum utilized a system of index cards and a classification system known as the Universal Decimal Classification (UDC) to categorize information. Otlet envisioned the Mundaneum as a precursor to the modern internet, where people could access information from anywhere in the world. Although the Mundaneum faced challenges and eventually declined, its vision of a global information network laid the groundwork for future developments in information science and technology.

Source: Wikipedia
