History of the Web

The World Wide Web was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 while working at CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research. The web was created to facilitate the sharing of information among scientists and researchers across the globe. Berners-Lee developed the first web browser and web server, laying the foundation for the modern internet. The web quickly gained popularity, leading to the development of websites, online services, and e-commerce platforms that have transformed how we communicate, access information, and conduct business.
